About AgriSmart

A plain-language explanation of the problem we solved, the data we collected, and the machine learning behind every recommendation.

The Problem

Selecting the wrong crop for a season can mean months of work with little return. Farmers across India often rely on local habit rather than data — leading to missed revenue opportunities. We built AgriSmart to replace guesswork with a model-driven recommendation backed by decades of real agricultural records.

Data Sources

Three real-world datasets were cleaned, merged, and used to train the models:

  • Crop Production Records — District-level production data spanning multiple decades from government agricultural databases.
  • Rainfall Statistics — State-level seasonal rainfall, one of the strongest predictors of crop viability.
  • Wholesale Market Prices — Average prices per crop used to convert yield into projected revenue.
How the AI Works

Two machine learning models power every prediction:

  • Crop Classifier — A stacking ensemble of Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, and AdaBoost. Each model votes independently; a meta-model combines their outputs into a single recommendation.
  • Revenue Regressor — A Gradient Boosting model that estimates expected revenue per hectare, shown as a ±20% range to account for market variation.
