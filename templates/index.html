Project Overview

The Science of AgriSmart

An in-depth look at our methodology, data sourcing, and predictive architecture.

The Objective

Agricultural planning in India has historically been guided by tradition and seasonal patterns. However, as climate volatility and market fluctuations increase, farmers require more precise, data-driven insights. AgriSmart was developed to bridge this gap, providing a scientific foundation for crop selection.

Data Foundations

Our intelligence is derived from the integration of three primary data streams:

  • Historical Production: District-level data spanning decades to identify regional crop suitability.
  • Meteorological Trends: State-level rainfall data to account for seasonal moisture availability.
  • Economic Indicators: Wholesale market price indices to project potential revenue.

These disparate sources were normalized and synthesized to create a comprehensive training environment for our models.

Predictive Framework

We employ a dual-model architecture to provide both recommendations and financial projections:

  • Classification Stack: A sophisticated ensemble combining Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, and AdaBoost. This "stacking" approach ensures that the final recommendation is the result of consensus among multiple high-performance algorithms.
  • Revenue Regression: A Gradient Boosting regressor that calculates expected value per hectare, adjusted for historical yield and current market trends.
